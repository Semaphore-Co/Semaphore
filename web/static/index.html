Semaphore

Simpler, risk-free communication secured through the blockchain

Semaphore lets you express desires sincerely

In a digital world, words are cheap and authenticity is precious.
Semaphore helps you discover hidden connections with your peers. Find out if that crush is mutual. If it isn't, they never know you tried.
To keep things genuine, a small amount of money is committed to each match. The recipient can try to reciprocate with it or just cash out.

Semaphore lets you know someone's answer without having to ask the question

Semaphore automatically asks hypothetical questions relating to people you know
You can make groups, share questions with friends, give them the answer to let them make up questions, and more
If you have a question you don't want to ask directly, it's likely that it will fit in with the rest of the questions on Semaphore
Semaphore will let you send them the question and tell you the response, without them knowing it wasn't asked automatically

Semaphore takes privacy seriously

both for the asker

  • All of your interactions are stored in a distributed format on the blockchain, so no one from server admins to hackers can see them
  • We ensure that you can't tell which of your friends use Semaphore, so even if you're the only one, there is no way for the answerer to know

and for the answerer

  • Users are always told that the question could have been asked by someone else; that's part of the fun
  • Before joining a group, users are always shown what kinds of things will become public by joining

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What makes Semaphore more than "Tinder with a blockchain?"
    • Semaphore has fundamentally different goals than traditional dating apps. It intends to expand its scope beyond just romantic interest, but even within the realm of matching romantic partners, Semaphore is about “discovering hidden connections” rather than pairing up strangers. Tinder matches involve very little commitment, and many users find their time wasted with frivolous pairings. Semaphore focuses on people who already know each other and want to determine whether some feeling or belief is mutual.
  • Why require money for matches when existing services are free?
    • Offering up money serves adds a cost to Semaphore matches, which is an effective way to demonstrate that the signalled belief is genuine. Without this kind of commitment, there would be nothing stopping someone from signalling everything to everyone, just to see what responses they get back, and the whole system becomes unreliable. From an individual's perspective, spending a small amount of money will ensure that any response is genuine, a service that they can't get anywhere else.
  • Why would the average person have any interest in Semaphore?
    • Even if nothing else about the platform interests someone, there's always the lure of potential free money. A key feature of Semaphore is that users anonymously receive the money that other people commit when matching with them. Someone who is popular, well-connected, or attractive has a chance to earn money by signing up for Semaphore and evangelizing it to their peers. In this sense, Semaphore markets itself.
  • Doesn't Semaphore rely too heavily on "network effects" to be useful?
    • Not particularly. A user wishing to signal to someone without a Semaphore account provides some of that person's contact information, and they receive an explanation of what Semaphore is and instructions on signing up. The money they can claim provides an incentive for them to sign up and see what Semaphore is about. Additionaly, Semaphore doesn't provide users with information on who else has an account, so a large set of users is not needed to preserve anonymity.
  • Why is blockchain technology useful for this application?
    • As a distributed application, Semaphore is trustless. There is no private data stored in a central database. Instead, match information is stored on the Ethereum blockchain, with the potential for further obfuscation using zk-SNARKs. As countless hacks (such as Ashley Madison in 2015) have demonstrated, centralized databases are a liability when it comes to this kind of information.
  • Who's behind Semaphore?
    • Semaphore was imagined and designed by Dennis Maloney, a Computer Science and Economics student at MIT. Development of Semaphore is a collaboration between Dennis and Kent Slaney, another computer science student at MIT.
  • What's the meaning of the name Semaphore?
    • A semaphore is a signal, and Semaphore is a platform for sending reliable signals. Additionally, "semaphore" has a technical meaning within the realm of computer science. Computer programs are often split up into separate threads that run at the same time. A semaphore is a variable used to make sure that different threads don't access and change a common resource at the same time, which would cause problems in the program. Semaphore serves an analogous role: it controls access to information in a manner that ensures no one gets hurt.
  • Is there any significance behind the graphic on the front page?
    • Yes, actually. The public/private key algorithm typically used by blockchains relies on elliptic curve cryptography, the continuous version of which is illustrated by the graphic. If you're interested, you can read more about elliptic curve cryptography in this Cloudflare blog post.
  • Is Semaphore open source?
    • Yes, Semaphore's source code is available on GitHub.
